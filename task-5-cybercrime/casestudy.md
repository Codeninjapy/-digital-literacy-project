Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

Online payment fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information or making unauthorized transactions. One common method used is through fake
payment requests or phishing links.

In a typical scenario, a fraudster contacts a victim through a phone call, message, or social media platform pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or seller. The
attacker may send a fake link or request the victim to scan a QR code, claiming that it is required to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes
a payment from their own account.

Students and online users are often targeted because they frequently use digital payment apps and may not be fully aware of such scams. These attacks usually happen quickly, creating a
sense of urgency so that the victim does not think carefully before acting.

The consequences of such fraud include financial loss, stress, and misuse of personal information. In some cases, victims may also lose access to their bank accounts temporarily.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while using digital payment platforms. Users should never share OTPs, passwords, or click on unknown links, and should always
verify the source before making any transaction.
